About

Research that connects geospatial technology with environmental decisions.

I am a Postdoctoral Fellow in the Department of Geography and Environmental Studies at Wilfrid Laurier University and Administrative Manager of the Cold Regions Research Centre.

My research focuses on the use of Earth observation, spatial analysis, machine learning, cloud computing, and high-performance computing to study northern lakes, water quality, lake thermal dynamics, ice phenology, and environmental change.

I also teach GIS, cartography, raster analysis, remote sensing, and climate change, with an emphasis on practical skills, critical thinking, and reproducible workflows.

Research areas

Where my work is concentrated

01

Freshwater Remote Sensing

Monitoring lake temperature, turbidity, water quality, and seasonal dynamics using multi-sensor satellite observations.

02

Lake Ice and Thermal Dynamics

Examining lake ice thickness, freeze-up, break-up, ice-cover duration, and long-term climate responses across northern landscapes.

03

GeoAI and Environmental Modelling

Developing scalable machine-learning workflows that integrate satellite imagery, climate data, in-situ measurements, and spatial models.

04

Climate and Community Resilience

Producing actionable spatial information to support environmental monitoring, northern communities, and evidence-based decision-making.

Great Slave Lake

Remote sensing · Machine learning · Water quality

Spatiotemporal Turbidity Dynamics in Great Slave Lake

Developed a seamless MODIS–VIIRS turbidity dataset and dual-regime random forest workflow to examine spatial patterns, seasonal variability, long-term trends, and hydroclimatic controls across Great Slave Lake.

Lake ice · Climate change · Spatial modelling

Lake Ice Thickness and Phenology Across 535 Northern Lakes

Produced multi-decadal lake ice thickness, freeze-up, break-up, and ice-cover duration estimates using Landsat-derived lake surface temperature, ERA5 climate data, and a spatially distributed modelling framework.
